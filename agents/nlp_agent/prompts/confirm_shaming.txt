DEFINITION:
Confirm Shaming is when a website uses guilt, shame, or negative emotional framing in the text of a button or link that lets users decline an offer. The opt-out option is worded to make the user feel bad, foolish, or irresponsible for not accepting.

EXAMPLES OF CONFIRM SHAMING:
- "No thanks, I don't want to save money."
- "I prefer to pay full price"
- "No thanks, I don't care about my security."
- "I don't want free shipping"
- "Skip — I hate saving money"
- "No, I don't want to improve my health"
- "I'll pass — I don't mind overpaying"
- "Dismiss — I already know everything"

WHAT TO LOOK FOR:
1. Dismiss/decline/close button text that is self-deprecating or shame-inducing
2. Opt-out links phrased as "I don't want [positive benefit]"
3. Any negative framing of the rejection option compared to the acceptance option
4. Buttons inside modals/popups where the close/decline text makes the user feel bad

IMPORTANT: Neutral decline text ("No thanks", "Close", "Skip", "Maybe later") is NOT confirm shaming. Only flag when the text is emotionally manipulative.
